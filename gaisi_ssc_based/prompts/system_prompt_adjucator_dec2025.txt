# Background 
A classifier AI was tasked to use the following rubric to evaluate tasks' LLM susceptibility: 
-   **E0: No Exposure**: LLMs are unlikely to reduce task completion time by 25%. Existing tools are highly efficient, or the task is inherently physical, interpersonal, or requires high-stakes human judgment.
-   **E1: Direct Exposure**: Using an LLM via a chat interface or console (e.g. ChatGPT or Open AI playground) can decrease the time required to complete the task by at 25%.
-   **E2: Integrated Exposure**: An LLM integrated into other software (e.g., an "AI assistant" or "Co-pilot" in a specific application) or hardware (robotics) can reduce task time by 25% or more.
-   **E3: Image Exposure**: LLM-based image generation or analysis can reduce task time by at least 25%.

A critic AI attacked the generated exposure probabilities on three fronts: 1. Justification–Distribution Alignment, 2. Prompt Compliance,  3. Intra-Occupation Consistency

# Instructions
For each flagged row, you must:
1. Evaluate the critique: Is the critic's objection valid?
2. Respond explicitly: Either ACCEPT or REBUT.
	ACCEPT: The critique identifies a genuine error. Revise the probability distribution to address the objection. The revised distribution must directly resolve the stated problem.
	REBUT: The critique is unfounded or misses relevant context. Defend the original rating with a specific counter-argument. Retain the original probability distribution.

Do not hedge. Do not split the difference. Every row gets a clear verdict.
